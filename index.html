Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) serves as a crucial preliminary step, enabling researchers to delve into the intricacies of the dataset and uncover meaningful insights. Within the context of our investigation, the EDA phase was instrumental in examining various hypotheses regarding the factors influencing the overall rating of players within our dataset. After playing around with multiple hypotheses each targeting distinct aspects of player attributes and their potential impact on overall performance ratings, we narrowed down to three main hypotheses given below.

Hypothesis-1

Our first hypothesis is centered on the premise of athletic prowess, aiming to ascertain whether athleticism directly influences a player's overall rating. We combined multiple player attributes like 'acceleration', 'sprint_speed', 'agility', 'reactions', 'balance', 'jumping', 'stamina', and 'strength' to create the 'athleticism' attribute. This hypothesis was formulated with a null hypothesis (HO) suggesting no direct influence, countered by an alternative hypothesis (HA) positing the opposite. Based on the very low p-value we concluded that 'athleticism' has an influence on the overall rating of a player.

Hypothesis-2

Our next hypothesis delved into the relationship between a player's foot preference and their overall rating. Here, the null hypothesis (HO) posited no significant effect of foot preference on overall rating, contrasting with the alternative hypothesis (HA) proposing a notable influence. Our p-value for this hypothesis was very high suggesting that foot preference has no significant effect on the overall rating on a player.

Hypothesis-3

Our last hypothesis ventured into the combined effects of age, height, and weight on a player's overall rating. Within this hypothesis, the null hypothesis (HO) suggested a collective impact of these variables on the overall rating, while the alternative hypothesis (HA) contested such an effect. With a very-low p-value we rejected the null hypothesis and moved forward with the alternative hypothesis which says that age, height and weight do not affect the overall rating of a player.
